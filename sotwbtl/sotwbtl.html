Stars on their way Back to Light!

Due to extensive air and light pollution, especially in Lahore, we had lost the wonderful glimpses of the "chunks of Heaven" in the vast, gargantuan sky.
But, we have been granted one more chance to save the art of stargazing. Yes, I am referring to one of the benifits that lockdown has brought us. (Not to mention the cancellation of board exams. PARTY HARD!!!... Alone LMAO!)
The stars are fighting back! They have turned the tide against the artificial light and have yet again begun to fill our eyes with their charm and allure.
I'll quote my uncle here; he told me, "When we were your age, we could see the Milky Way galaxy! We would scuttle off to our roofs at night to see the awe-inspiring spectacle, especially in September, and sometimes even went to sleep while stargazing. Your grandparents had to kick us up and it hurt a lot, but it was worth it!"
Have to admit I was really jealous. I always wanted to follow the star-trails through the aperture of my camera's lens.
Well... the news that makes us frolic is, (DRUM ROLL) "The stars are back in business!" I really hope they continue spreading beauty everywhere succeeding lockdown and the end of these disastrous times. May the skies remain as starry as they are meant to be. (Amen)
Also , may all the sick be cured. (Amen)
Prayers be with you... Adios!
